# What is Digital Youth Work?

Digital youth work refers to the use of digital tools, platforms, and environments to support young people’s learning, participation, wellbeing, and development.

It includes both:
- **Online youth work** (e.g. Zoom sessions, Discord groups, social media engagement)
- **Digital approaches in physical settings** (e.g. workshops using apps, games, or creative tools)

## In the context of CLEVER

CLEVER focuses on supporting youth workers and educators to deliver digital youth work that is:

### Safe
Young people are protected, supported, and aware of risks in digital spaces.

### Resilient
Young people develop the skills to manage challenges, respond to issues, and make informed decisions online.

### Participatory
Young people actively engage, create, and contribute in positive ways.

### Creative (future phase)
Young people explore digital creativity through tools such as games, AI, and immersive technologies.

## What this includes
- Online safety and digital wellbeing
- Group work in online spaces
- Platform awareness (e.g. gaming, social media)
- Creative digital activities
- Youth-led participation

## What this does not include
- Unmoderated or unsupervised engagement
- Collection or sharing of personal data
- Activities without safeguarding consideration

---

CLEVER is designed as a practical, open resource to support real-world youth work practice in digital environments.
